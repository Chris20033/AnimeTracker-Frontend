# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AnimeTracker is for anime viewers who want a personal place to track what they are watching, what they plan to watch, what they completed, and what they consider favorites. Users may arrive unauthenticated to register, log in, or recover access, then work inside authenticated flows such as dashboard, profile, search, library, favorites, and statistics.

## Product Purpose

AnimeTracker exists to make anime tracking feel organized, personal, and easy to resume. The MVP succeeds when a user can create an account, recover access, manage a profile, search anime, add entries to a personal library, update progress and status, mark favorites, and read basic personal statistics without the interface feeling generic or confusing.

## Brand Personality

Editorial anime, modern, calm. The product should feel intentionally designed for anime tracking: expressive enough to have character, restrained enough to remain useful as an everyday tool, and clear enough that forms, lists, dashboard surfaces, and future library workflows stay fast to understand.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, excessive neon/cyberpunk styling, washed-out low-contrast gray text, decorative gradient text, glassmorphism as the default, repeated identical card grids, and AI-looking section scaffolds such as tiny uppercase eyebrows on every block. The interface should not look like a template unrelated to anime or like an overstimulating fan site.

## Design Principles

- Make tracking feel resumable: every screen should help users understand where they are and what action comes next.
- Add anime character with restraint: use visual identity to support the product, not distract from workflows.
- Prioritize clear states: loading, success, error, empty, authenticated, and unauthenticated states should be explicit.
- Keep future growth in mind: dashboard, library, search, favorites, profiles, and statistics should share a coherent system.
- Protect trust: auth and password recovery flows should feel secure, calm, and predictable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline. Preserve visible focus states, keyboard-accessible controls, readable contrast in light and dark mode, labels for inputs, accessible names for icon-only buttons, and reduced-motion alternatives for meaningful animation. Do not rely only on color to communicate state.
